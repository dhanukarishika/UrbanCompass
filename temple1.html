Kapaleeswarar Temple




Dedicated to the Arulmigu Kapaleeswarar incarnation of Lord Shiva and Karpagambal incarnation of his wife, Goddess Parvati, Kapaleeswarar Temple is one of the most visited Shiva temples in Chennai. Situated in Vinayak Nagar Colony of Chennai, the temple brings forth the intricate style of Dravidian architecture. The interiors of the temple are adorned with carvings of wood and stone, decorated pillars and the Gopuram doorway. Conceived in the 7th Century by the Pallavas, the temple receives visitors throughout the year from all over the country. Location: The temple is situated in the Kapaleeswarar, Kesava Perumal Sannathi St Vinayaka Nagar Colony in Mylapore, Chennai, Tamil Nadu 600004 Timings: The temple is open to visitors from 5:30 AM to 12:00 PM and 5:00 PM to 9:00 PM from Tuesday to Sunday.



Parthasarathy Temple



Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the supreme household deity of the Hindu mythology, Parthasarathy Temple is the 61st Divya Desam temple out of the famous 108 Vaishnava temples. Established during the reign of Pallavas, the temple is one of the oldest temples in the city. Its interiors are laden with carvings and inscriptions of the ancient era while its exteriors proudly showcase a Dravidian style of architecture. The temple is famous for being a resting place from pilgrims and travellers for a thousand years. It holds major religious significance among worshippers and is visited by hundreds of people every day. Location: The temple is located at Narayana Krishnaraja Puram in Triplicane, Chennai, Tamil Nadu 600005. Timings: The temple is open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 12:30 PM and 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM.


Parrys Jain Temple



-

Dedicated to Sri Chandraprabhu Bhagwan, the 8th Tirthankara of the Jain tradition, the Parrys Jain temple is one of the several Jain places of worship located in Chennai. Despite being a Hindu-oriented holy city, Chennai is home to temples of all faiths. Parrys Jain Temple is a quaint temple with simplistic Jain style architecture. Its exteriors reflect the atmosphere inside the temple as the facade is decorated with pristine white marble. The surface of the building is also adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions. The temple attracts thousands of believers from all across the country and is a testament to the universality of the Jain culture. If you are looking for temples to visit in Chennai, the Parrys Jain Temple is an excellent choice. Location: The temple is situated in 142, Mini Street, Snowcarpet, George Town in Chennai.

Ekambareswarar Temple



Located in the heart of Kanchipuram. Ekambareswarar Temple is a famous Hindu place of worship dedicated to Lord Shiva, the supreme deity of the Hindu faith. The temple is held in very high religious regard as it is one of the five extraordinary temples that represent a universal element. The Ekambareswarar Temple represents the element of earth explicitly. With its exteriors boasting of skilled craftsmanship and heavy Dravidian influence, the temple building is an elegantly high-rising structure covered in earth colours. The temple is hugely revered by locals and attracts thousands of tourists every year. Location: The temple is located in Ekambaranathar Sannidhi Street, in Periya Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, 631502. Timings: The temple is open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 12:30 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:30 PM.



Vadapalani Andavar Temple




Located in the Vadapalani region of Tamil Nadu, Andavar Temple is a sacred Hindu site dedicated to Lord Muruga. Lord Muruga, widely known as Lord Kartikeya, was the son of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Built-in the 19th Century, the temple is famous for its long-lasting wedding traditions. It is believed that over 7000 couples marry in this temple each year. Couples travel to this temple to seek domestic blessings and boons from the presiding deity. The colourful exteriors of the temple reflect the bliss and happiness that its worship promises. Location: The temple is located at Vadapalani in Chennai, Tamil Nadu.
